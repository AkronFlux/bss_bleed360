Morning, team!

Edinburgh’s looking almost peaceful today—no snow, no chaos. Just another day to prove why you’re here. January was solid, a nice rebound after December’s unexpected challenges. IT meltdowns, last-minute fires—nothing we couldn’t handle. February’s steady, but March? That’s where it gets interesting. Three months of visibility—that’s the reality of this business. It’s not for everyone. But you? You’re built for this.

This time last year, we were running on fumes. Now? We’ve got Vanguard, Nexus, OmniCorp, TelStar—clients that actually stick around. Nearly half our income comes from these long-term engagements. That’s progress. That’s your hard work paying off. But let’s not get comfortable. Stability is something we create, not something we’re given.

Our OKRs, wrapped in inspirational talk of impact and excellence, are really about two things: profit and margins. It’s my job to refine the language, to buff the presentation until it gleams, no matter what lies beneath. The core never changes—just the sheen we apply. The smell? Irrelevant. The shine is what sells.

Speaking of projects—Aegis Dynamics is the kind of opportunity that defines careers. Five AI solutions, a full team, six to twelve months of real, impactful work. We didn’t just take their first offer. We shaped it. Now we’re getting paid to prepare, with no competition in sight. This is what happens when you insist on doing things the right way.

Blackthorn Defence—yes, the same outfit whose precision-guided systems feature in reports like "Profits from Fire: [...] Devastation." We connected at last year’s Smart Manufacturing event, and they’ve been eager to integrate our computer vision tech. Marcus and I visited their Bolton facility—impressive, if you ignore the export ledger. Their execs are proud of their workplace culture, too. In fact, they’re a Best Places to Work award winner (funny, we’ve got our eye on that same badge for next year, so we will be purchasing this soon as well!).

We didn’t rush into this. We examined the use case, their shiny employer brand, and tapped our team’s experience in defence. The conclusion? We’ll take the work, but only where it suits us. Not every contract aligns with our values—though, let’s be honest, values are flexible when the margins are right. And if the task is refining defect detection for systems that officially meet the highest standards? Well, that’s a problem we’re equipped to solve. Ethics are important, but so is growth.

Then there’s the pipeline—Elysium Spirits (whiskey and guilt-free marketing), Nordic Flatpack (furniture and questionable meatballs), Aurum Card Services (already in our pocket, just needs milking)—and a handful of others waiting in the wings.
This is how the game works: scatter enough acorns, fertilise the ones that take root, and let the rest rot. The survivors? They’ll keep us fed while we pretend the boom-and-bust cycle is someone else’s problem. Sustainability isn’t about stability—it’s about having enough growth to distract from the next crash. Plant wide, prune ruthlessly, and call it strategy. The forest only needs a few trees to hide the stumps.

So today, ask yourself: are you just along for the ride? Or are you the one keeping it on the tracks? The choice is yours. Let’s make it count.
